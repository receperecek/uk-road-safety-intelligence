# Light Conditions Findings v1

## Key Insights
- Daylight accounted for the highest collision volume, with 359,837 collisions, representing 71.47% of decoded light-condition records.
- Darkness with lights lit was the second-largest segment by collision volume, with 104,155 collisions.
- Darkness with no lighting showed the strongest severity profile, with a fatal collision rate of 4.91% and a KSI collision rate of 34.24%.
- This indicates that the most common lighting environment is not the most severe one from an outcome perspective.

## Analyst Interpretation
Lighting conditions show a clear separation between exposure-heavy and severity-heavy environments. Daylight dominates total collision volume, which is consistent with normal traffic exposure patterns. However, collisions in darkness without road lighting appear substantially more severe when they occur. This suggests that lighting environment should be treated as a severity driver rather than only a contextual descriptor.

## Business Relevance
This finding strengthens the decision-support case for targeted safety strategy:
- daylight conditions dominate operational collision volume
- unlit darkness conditions carry disproportionate severity risk
- lighting context is a strong candidate feature for downstream severity scoring and intervention prioritization
