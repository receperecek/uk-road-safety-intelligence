# Road Type Findings v1

## Key Insights
- Single carriageway roads accounted for the highest collision volume, with 274,367 collisions, representing 72.66% of decoded road-type records.
- Single carriageway roads also showed the highest KSI collision rate at 25.00%, making them the most important road-type segment when frequency and severity are considered together.
- Dual carriageway roads recorded a lower collision share than single carriageways, but a higher fatal collision rate at 2.05%, suggesting a different risk profile.
- Roundabouts showed a comparatively lower fatal collision rate at 0.48%, indicating less severe outcomes relative to the main through-road categories.

## Analyst Interpretation
Road type should not be interpreted only through collision volume. Single carriageway roads emerge as the dominant operational priority because they combine scale and severity. Dual carriageways, however, appear to carry a sharper fatality profile despite lower overall collision volume. This suggests that intervention design should vary by road form rather than relying on a single national risk lens.

## Business Relevance
This finding strengthens the decision-support case for segmented road safety strategy:
- single carriageways are strong candidates for broad priority intervention
- dual carriageways may require targeted severity-reduction measures
- roundabouts may provide useful contrast when identifying lower-severity road environments

## Scope Note
The 'Unknown' road type category is excluded from interpretation priority and should not drive policy-facing conclusions.
