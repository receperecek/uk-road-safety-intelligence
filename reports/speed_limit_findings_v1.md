# Speed Limit Findings v1

## Key Insights
- 30 mph roads accounted for the highest collision volume, with 269,912 collisions, representing 53.61% of all interpretable speed-limit records.
- 60 mph roads showed the highest severity burden, with a 33.76% KSI collision rate and a 3.93% fatal collision rate.
- 20 mph roads had a materially lower fatal collision rate at 0.45%, indicating lower-severity outcomes relative to higher-speed segments.
- The core analytical takeaway is that collision volume is concentrated in 30 mph environments, while collision severity intensifies as speed limits rise, especially on 60 mph roads.

## Analyst Interpretation
This pattern suggests that road safety strategy should distinguish between high-volume urban exposure and high-severity higher-speed corridors. In practice, 30 mph roads may require broad exposure-focused safety measures, while 60 mph segments may warrant targeted severity-reduction interventions.

## Business Relevance
This finding supports a decision-support view of road safety:
- volume hotspots do not automatically equal severity hotspots
- intervention priorities should be ranked using both frequency and severity
- speed context is a strong candidate feature for downstream risk scoring and hotspot prioritization
