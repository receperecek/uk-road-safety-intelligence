# Urban Rural Findings v1

## Key Insights
- Urban areas accounted for the majority of collisions, with 269,491 collisions, representing 67.51% of decoded urban-rural records.
- Rural areas represented a smaller share of total collision volume at 32.48%, but showed materially higher severity outcomes.
- Rural collisions recorded a fatal collision rate of 2.87%, compared with 0.82% in urban areas.
- Rural collisions also showed a higher KSI collision rate at 28.77%, versus 20.72% in urban areas.

## Analyst Interpretation
This result shows a clear separation between exposure concentration and severity concentration. Urban areas dominate total collision volume, likely due to higher traffic density and exposure. Rural environments, however, appear significantly more severe when collisions occur. This suggests that collision frequency and collision harm should not be treated as the same planning problem.

## Business Relevance
This finding supports a segmented safety strategy:
- urban areas may require volume-reduction and exposure-management interventions
- rural areas may require severity-reduction interventions
- urban-rural classification is a strong candidate feature for downstream severity scoring and hotspot prioritization
